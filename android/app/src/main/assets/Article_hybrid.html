French law prohibits supermarkets to waste food

Vr 22 mei, 13:22
World
AFP

French supermarkets are no longer allowed to throw away expired food. The French parliament has adopted a law that forces supermarkets to find alternative destinations for overstocked groceries.

They could donate it to a charity, process it in energy plants for green energy or use it as food for livestock.

The plan was adopted unanimously. According to French media initiator Guillaume Garot said it was outrageous that bleach is poured into the same trash can that contains edible food.

Contract

The law strictly prohibits destroying edible food. Large supermarkets, with over 400 square meters of floor size, have to sign contracts with a charity before june. Otherwise they could face a fine of 75.000 euros.

The French association of retailers is firmly against the new law.
